# Lesson 1: Introduction to Whole Numbers

---

## 1. Counting Numbers vs Whole Numbers

These are called **natural numbers** because we naturally use them.

Counting starts with:
1, 2, 3, 4, 5…

If we add 0, we get **whole numbers**:
0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5…

### Key ideas:
- Counting numbers start at **1**
- Whole numbers start at **0**
- The sequence continues forever: …

---

## 2. Place Value System

Every digit in a number has a value based on its position.

Example: 5,278,194

- 5 = 5 million  
- 2 = 200,000  
- 7 = 70,000  
- 8 = 8,000  
- 1 = 100  
- 9 = 90  
- 4 = 4  

### Key idea:
A digit’s value depends on its position.

### Periods:
- Ones
- Thousands
- Millions
- Billions
- Trillions (and so on)

---

## 3. Writing Numbers in Words

Numbers can be written in two ways:
- Digits: 74,218,369
- Words: seventy-four million…

### Rule:
Start from the left (largest value) and move right.

---

## 4. Rounding Whole Numbers

Rounding makes numbers simpler but still close.

Example:
19,651,127 ≈ 20,000,000

### Rules:
- 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 → round down
- 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 → round up
- Replace digits to the right with zeros

---

## 5. Multiples of Numbers

A multiple is created by multiplying a number by 1, 2, 3, 4…

Example: Multiples of 2
2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12…

### Key idea:
Multiples are results of repeated multiplication.

---

## 6. Divisibility Tests

Quick rules to check if a number divides evenly.

### Divisible by 2
Last digit is: 0, 2, 4, 6, 8

### Divisible by 5
Last digit is: 0 or 5

### Divisible by 10
Last digit is: 0

### Divisible by 3
Sum of digits is divisible by 3

### Divisible by 6
Divisible by both 2 and 3

---

## 7. Factors and Prime Numbers

A factor divides a number exactly.

Example: 72
1 × 72  
2 × 36  
3 × 24  
4 × 18  
6 × 12  
8 × 9  

### Prime numbers
- Only 2 factors: 1 and itself  
- Example: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11…

### Composite numbers
- More than 2 factors  
- Example: 12

---

## 8. Prime Factorization

Breaking a number into **only prime numbers**.

Example:
48 = 6 × 8  
6 = 2 × 3  
8 = 2 × 2 × 2  

So:
48 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3  

### Key rule:
Final answer must contain only prime numbers.

---

## 9. Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The LCM is the smallest number both numbers divide into.

Example:
- 12 → 12, 24, 36, 48…
- 18 → 18, 36, 54…

LCM = 36

---

## 10. LCM Using Prime Factors

Example:
12 = 2 × 2 × 3  
18 = 2 × 3 × 3  

Take highest powers:
2 × 2 × 3 × 3  

LCM = 36

### Key idea:
Use all prime factors with highest frequency.
